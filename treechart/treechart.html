Tree Chart

Examining the hierarchy of sample news coverage categories of The New York Times

Tree

This tree chart is using almost exactly the same data from the cluster chart to focus on highlighting the rather minor difference between the two charts (some data was changed to further highlight it; see Boroughs not having a child now). Again, this means the chart is displaying the hierarchy of how a small sample of different topics are categorized in the news. A legend was omitted, as the color hue is primarily used to help display the level in the hierarchy

Tree Chart Uses

The tree chart has many similarities to the cluster chart. It's still being used to show a visual hierarchy in a node based system with parents/children. However, some differences exist, the most important one being that the leafs are not put onto the same level regardless of their depth. In the tree map, leafs are placed and end where their natural depth is, as can be seen in how the "Boroughs" node ends at the same depth as the "Asia" node below it, despite not having a child leaf. The tree chart can also have different use cases, such as being used for instructions or procedures, to show potential steps that are shorter or longer in comparison to others, and knowing exactly where certain steps end in comparison to others.
