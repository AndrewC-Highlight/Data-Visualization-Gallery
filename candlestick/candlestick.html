Candlestick Chart

The candlestick chart is one of the more visually interesting charts for a very specific purpose: financial measurements. This particular chart analyzes real data for the NYT Stock price from 9/30/19 - 11/01/19. The chart shows the a recap of the price movement on the weekdays. Looking at one candlestick tick, the top line/shadow displays the high for the day, the bottom line/shadow displays the low from that day, while the colored body shows the open and close measurement. Color is added for ease of reading, with the color green signifying a higher close than open, and the red color showing a lower close than open. Data found: https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/NYT/history?p=NYT

Choropleth Map Uses

The candlestick chart is a great way to analyze price movements over a period of time. It can show trading patterns, with displaying the open, close, high, and low for each day. At a glance it can show the overall pattern of the price over time, and with further delving can show the user where specifically the four pieces of information land and trend at. While specifically used for financial charts (movements of stocks, securities, currencies), it provides a clear and efficient way of analyze patterns.

Marks and Channels

  • Marks: Lines (shadows for the high and low), Rectangle (the body for the open and close).
  • Channels: Color Hue (signifying the value of the close vs open), Length (amount of movement in a day), Position
