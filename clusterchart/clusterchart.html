Cluster Chart

Examining the hierarchy of sample news coverage categories of The New York Times

The Cluster Chart

This cluster chart uses the same made-up data from the force graph, but in a different way. It forgoes the interactivity, and instead establishes the visual hierarchy through a series of parents and children with connecting lines to display the relationship. It pushes leaf nodes at the same depth as well. Again this chart is showing just a few categories and subcategories of news coverage.
I chose not to include a legend here, as I didn't think it necessarily needed it, with the color hue just helping identify the depth of a node.

Cluster Chart Uses

The cluster chart/dendrogram is similar to a force graph's goals, but executed differrently. It is a hierarchy tree representing different clustering relationships. A series of parents and children form the tree, and this graph can be useful for such things as evolution clusters, animal groups, or code library dependencies.
