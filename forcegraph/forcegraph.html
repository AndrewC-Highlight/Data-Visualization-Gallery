Force Graph

Examining the hierarchy of sample news coverage categories of The New York Times

The Force Graph

The force graph here demonstrates the relationships and flow of a sample dataset about the categories that the New York Times covers and subcategories. It is another made up list, and is not meant to be a full representation of coverage, only to show where the categories are ultimately coming from.

Force Graph Uses

The force graph is a way to draw a visually pleasing, and interactive, graph where a set of nodes are connected with a set edges. The nodes typically have forces and draggability to provide the interactivty. The graph can be useful to show relationships and hierarchies, especially connections between people or variables. It's also a ton of fun to wiggle around everywhere.
