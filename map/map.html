Map

This map showcases the top 50 US cities alongside showing if they're a bureau for The New York Times. The general idea of a map chart is to display data that is relevant to a geographic region. In this example only a few things were changed from the in class exercise, as I wanted to stay on my overall projects theme without designating a large amount of time to creating a new dataset.

Map Chart Uses

Maps are among the most common types of data visualizations, and contain a ton of variety (including a Choropleth being a type of map). Dealing with geographic data, maps can show various locations from the world to local counties. Popular uses include population counts, energy maps, flight maps, network maps, and more. There are tons of variety in maps that can serve almost any purpose.

Marks and Channels

  • Marks: Area (the U.S. map), Points (cities)
  • Channels: Color Hue (signifying bureau status), Size (proportional to population), Position
