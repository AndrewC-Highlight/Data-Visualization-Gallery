Choropleth Map

This choropleth map uses some (very fake) data displaying the percentage of a state's population who are registered users to any New York Times media or digital product. It ranges from 2-20%, and is solely depicting the United States.

Choropleth Map Uses

The choropleth map displays a certain geographical area utilizing quantitative data to differentiate sections within the map. Here, the states are the different sections that are having their color hue changed to represent a quantity. Choropleth maps are a strong visualization for displaying data that is measured across geographic regions, and can help in comparing how one region differentiates from the other. With numerous uses, choropleths are typical for showing income, population-based metrics, and can be used in a variant heat map.

Marks and Channels

  • Marks: Areas (the states/regions)
  • Channels: Color Hue (signifying the associated quantity)
